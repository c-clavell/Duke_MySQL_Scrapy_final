Best sports coupes

We’ve already dropped some pretty prestigious names, but now let’s take a quick look at what they each bring to the table in our best sports coupes rankings.

  • Mercedes-Benz - The AMG division of the German manufacturer has earned two spots within our top 10 with the S63 and E53 sport coupe cars. There’s about a $100k price difference between the two, with the former receiving a 603-horsepower twin-turbo V8, while the latter gets a 429-hp turbocharged inline-six. Both are great performers, but the S63 is quicker with a 3.4-second 0-60 mph sprint time.
  • BMW - The M8 Coupe is the top contender on the US market. Its twin-turbocharged V8 uses its 600 ponies to get the burly machine up to speed in just 3.1 seconds.
  • Lexus - The premium division of Toyota presents shoppers with the LC model in either 500 Coupe or Hybrid configuration. The gasoline version uses its V8 engine to make the 0-60 sprint in 4.4 seconds, while the V6 hybrid needs an extra 0.3 seconds.
  • Bentley - The Continental GT is a cumbersome beast, but thanks to its potent twin-turbo W12 engine, it can launch its hefty weight up to 60 mph in just 3.6 seconds even though it comes standard with AWD.

2-door Sports Cars vs 4-door

While there are certainly a lot of four-door sedans out there, nothing evokes the image of pure athleticism like a sleek two-door sports car. The sloping roofline of the 2-door sport car lends itself to a more minimalist design, so it only makes sense to streamline the rest of the exterior, too. Besides, when you purchase an automobile designed to give you whiplash, you probably aren’t planning to put the kids in the back. In fact, most of these high-performance machines are not meant for more than a single spectator, even if they do provide rear seats. The joy these high-priced toys bring comes from the hands-on experience; it’s not really a spectator activity.

FAQs about sports coupes

What are the best sporty coupes?

This is not a simple question. The sheer number of capable performance vehicles out there is quite staggering, and each has its own charm and appeal. We might score the Mercedes-AMG S63 Coupe higher than its rivals, but the BMW M8, Audi R8, Bentley Continental GT, and Porsche 911 Turbo all earn a BuzzScore of over nine points.

Are there any affordable sports coupes?

While the majority of sports coupe cars are aimed at the 1%, there are a number of “cheap” models for sale, too. Ford has the Mustang Bullitt, with a starting price of $46,705, while the Audi TT Coupe asks for $45,500, and the Toyota GR Supra has a round $40k price tag. Dodge is particularly well-known for supplying high-powered automobiles at a reasonable cost. If you have your heart set on one of the more famous nameplates, you will have to steer away from the 2022 models and rather buy a used model from 2021 or further back.
